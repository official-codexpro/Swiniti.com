Our Theory of Changes

At Swaniti, we believe that tackling climate change requires more than just bold targets… it demands systems that works for everyone, especially those most vulnerable to its impacts We understand that climate change is not a future possibility – it is a present crisis, and its consequences are not evenly distributed. Communities in the Global South, particularly those already living on the margins, are bearing the brunt of an accelerating climate emergency. From coal miners facing issues with just transition, to industries struggling to access green finances, and farmers experiencing low yields due to erratic weather, all of these are deepening existing vulnerabilities and testing the resilience of governance systems.

Why Energy Transition Efforts Falter on the Ground +

While national and international commitments to clean energy and net-zero goals are growing, implementation often falters on the ground because:

  • Limited technical capacity at the sub-national level
  • A lack of locally contextualised transition frameworks
  • Insufficient tools to monitor, evaluate, and deliver impact

This is where Swaniti begins its work.

Beginning Where It Matters Most: The Community+

We start by engaging with those who are most affected – local leaders, frontline workers, and community members. Their insights are not supplementary; they are central. Our first steps involve:

  • Listening to community experiences and aspirations
  • Mapping local assets and capacities
  • Identifying underutilized public resources that can enable a inclusive transition

These insights inform a deeper understanding of what climate equity should look like on the ground.

Co-creating Solutions from Within Communities +

We bring out solutions from within communities—By building a collaborative ecosystem that unlocks public funds and resources and combines it with local partners and community leaders we build out sustainable solutions.

Embedding Technical Capacity for Real-time Action+

Then comes the implementation. Our technical teams work closely with sub-national governments. We are embedded within departments to support real-time planning, problem-solving and implementation. From enabling solar deployment and improving climate finance access, to integrating resilience planning and building local data systems. we help convert intent into impact.

For us, climate action is not just about reducing emissions; it’s about building institutional capacity (unlocking public funds and resources), empowering communities (bringing change through increased representation), and enabling transformation (enabling action from key stakeholders) that reaches the last mile.
